Plotting territorial income distribution in the city of Barcelona

The objective of this article is to analyze and plot choropleth maps of territorial income distribution in the city of Barcelona and other demographic indicators obtained from Barcelona open data repository using Python's Folium library. Here we only present the final results, detailed technical implementation explaination is available in my Jupyter Notebook The first dataset that we are going to analyze is the Renta Familiar Disponible (RFD) index dataset, this dataset contains the Available Family Income Index by Neighborhood. Neighborhoods are colored from yellow to red according to their RFD index, as illustrated by the colorscale on top of the map. The RFD index of Barcelona city is 100, therefore neighborhoods with RFD index lower than 100 represent that their RFD index are below average of the city. Choropleth map on the right uses two different colorscale to better differentiate neighborhoods with lower than average RFD index with neighborhoods with equall or higher than average RFD index.
